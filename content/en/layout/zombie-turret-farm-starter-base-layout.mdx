<Section icon={Swords} title="Common Layout Mistakes to Avoid">

Even with a solid **zombie turret farm starter base layout**, certain tactical errors can stall your progress. Many players fall into the trap of "over-rolling," where they spend all their money trying to get a Legendary turret while their current wall is too weak to kill basic zombies. This leads to a stagnation in income.

Another common mistake is "Gapped Placement." Some players place turrets at the very beginning and the very end of the lane, leaving the middle empty. This is inefficient because it allows zombies to recover or take longer to die, slowing down the rate at which new zombies spawn and are defeated.

| Mistake | Consequence | Solution |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Over-Rolling** | No money for upgrades | Spend 60% on upgrades, 40% on rolls |
| **Early Expansion** | Empty slots, zero DPS increase | Only expand when current slots are maxed |
| **Ignoring Rarity** | Stalled power scaling | Always swap low-tier for high-tier rolls |
| **Gapped Placement** | Inefficient kill times | Keep turrets in a tight, continuous wall |

<Callout type="warning" title="Upgrade Priority">
Never upgrade a Common turret to max level if you have an Uncommon or Rare turret available. Save your resources for units that have better long-term potential.
</Callout>
